# Child Cell Editing

Child cells help you break complex content into smaller editable units. Use them when a source segment contains multiple translation units, needs supporting paratextual content, or benefits from a more detailed structure.

## Split a Cell

1. Open the target cell.
2. Open the cell tools menu.
3. Choose the split action.
4. Edit the new child cells.
5. Save your changes.

## Add Supporting Content

Use supporting or paratextual cells for headings, section markers, notes, or other content that belongs near the translation but is not a direct source segment.

1. Open the relevant cell.
2. Open the cell tools menu.
3. Add a cell above or below.
4. Enter the supporting content.
5. Save your changes.

## Delete a Child Cell

1. Clear the child cell content.
2. Open the cell tools menu for that cell.
3. Choose the delete action.
4. Confirm the deletion.

<Callout type="warning">
Be careful when deleting child cells in shared projects. If other teammates have comments, recordings, or validations tied to the cell, coordinate before removing it.
</Callout>

## Related Tools

- **Locked cells** prevent accidental edits or recordings.
- **Milestones** help keep structured content navigable.
- **Comments** are better than extra child cells for discussion or questions.

See [Translation Tools](/docs/translation/translation-tools), [Milestones and Navigation](/docs/translation/milestones-and-navigation), and [Comments](/docs/translation/comments).
